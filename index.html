What's in a rose?

Breaking down February 14th's most popular gift.

Roses are a quintessential gift on Valentine's Day, and meaning has been attached to the popular flower for generations. But what does the rose actually signify? And does that meaning change with the color?

Red roses are the most popular gift for Valentine's Day because they represent love and beauty. Giving someone a pink rose means that you admire them. Yellow, meanwhile, depending on who you give it to, can mean friendship or infidelity.

Beyond roses, other flowers that work well as Valentine's Day gifts include:

  • pink peonies
  • white chrysanthemums
  • red tulips
  • purple lilac

Their meanings are varied, and each flower can send a very different message.
